
### **Primary Theme**

Hope, Self-Confidence, Redemption, Self-Agency

**The Lie**: Amara believes that she is beyond redemption and a failure; she is hopeless and has resigned herself to her fate.

**The Truth**: Amara learns that she can redeem herself, she regains hope, 


### **Act 1: Setup and Call-to-Action**

#### **Roles** 

**Sister Amara Ignatia (Protagonist)** 

- **Description:** Once a celebrated Canoness Superior, Amara (roughly 50 years old) must rise again after being demoted and disgraced. Strategic, patient, slow to anger, and intolerant of incompetence. 
- **Appearance:** Olive skin, shoulder-length gray bobbed hair with side swept bangs, lightly wrinkled but youthful for her age. Has a burn scar going from her ear going half way through her cheek. Her old silver armor is dented and scorched, adorned with frayed white-and-red tabard and purity seals. 
- **Weapons:** Eviscerator chainsword, inferno pistol, malfunctioning rosarius. 
- **Character Arch**: Hope, Self Confidence and Redemption

   
 

**Novitae Victoria Luminara (Deuteragonist)** 

- **Description:** A fiery, arrogant novice (age 20) eager to prove herself. Initially dismissive of Amara but grows to respect her. 
- **Appearance:** Pale skin, bleached, white, jaw-length hair with blunt bangs, polished silver armor with a saturated white-and-crimson tabard. The quintessential Sister of Battle. 
- **Weapons:** Flamer, devastating at close range. 
- **Character Arch**: Transformation from arrogance to respect and maturity. 

   
 

**Sister Justine Gladius** 

- **Description:** A tech-savvy Sister who trained with the Adeptus Mechanicus, often dismissed by her peers as annoying. 
- **Appearance:** Light brown skin, rebreather covering her mouth and nose, goggles usually on top of her head, mechanical third arm. Silver armor with soot and oil stains. 
- **Weapons:** Modified plasma rifle, safer for human use. 
- **Character Arch**: Proving her brilliance and value despite dismissal.


   
 

**Hospitaler Silva Florentia** 

- **Description:** The squad’s nurturing peacekeeper and medic. Tall and athletic but reluctant in combat. 
- **Appearance:** Tan skin, warm motherly face, pristine silver armor with an embroidered white-and-crimson tabard. She has a wimple with a fleur-de-lis in the center and the sleeve on her right arm is ripped off. Accompanied by a cherub assistant. 
- **Weapons:** Boltgun carbine, medical gauntlet with hypodermic needles. 
- **Character Arch**: Reconciliation of healer and warrior identities.

   
 

**Sister Superior Luciana Castella** 

- **Description:** Secretly corrupted by Tzeentch, orchestrates the Chaos invasion. Killed early by Amara. 
- **Appearance:** Tall, fair skin, scar across her nose, pristine white hair. Her armor shimmers with chaotic energy as her betrayal is revealed. 
- **Weapons:** Power sword that crackles with Warp energy. 

   
 

**Arcanus "The Summoner" Maleficarum (Antagonist)** 

- **Description:** A powerful psyker and former Ultramarine, now an agent of Tzeentch. An alpha-level psyker, skilled in summoning daemons and opening Warp gates. "something horrible happened to me, so if i become the chaos trickster god's bitch I can latch onto something to help me find some sort of control in the world. The emperor didn't save me or help me, so maybe tzeentch will."
- **Dark Mirror**: Lost in the dispair of his losses and failures, he turned to the gods of chaos and begged them fore power. He is a dark mirror to Amara, what a man who has lost all hope can become, a pawn for the gods of chaos.
- **Appearance:** His Terminator armor, once Ultramarine blue, now fused to his body and covered in shifting Warp glyphs. His blurred, unnatural face glows with purple flame. 
- **Weapons:** Chainglaive, both a melee weapon and a Warp conduit. 

   
 

**Chapters** 

**1. Amara’s Fall from Grace** 

- **Flashback:** Amara, in her prime, leads the Argent Shroud but suffers a catastrophic defeat due to her arrogance. Note the inquisitor that accompanied her on the assault testafies against her but also prevents Amara from being strapped to an Engine of Redemption.
- **Consequence:** Demoted to Sister Repentia. She survives years of grueling battles but feels unworthy of redemption. 
- **Present Day:** Assigned to Veridia, a desolate backwater planet. Amara, hopeless, contemplates suicide. She feels she will never redeem herself.

   
 

**2. Veridia and the Squad’s Introduction** 

- **Setting:** Veridia, a frigid, forgotten mining world with a small Sisters of Battle outpost of a few hundred. There are a some small Imperial Guard base nearby.
- **Key Introductions:** 

- **Luciana Castella:** Commanding but secretive leader. 
- **Victoria:** Brash and dismissive of Amara. 
- **Justine:** Annoyingly competent tech expert. 
- **Silva:** Kind but lacks combat confidence. 

- **Dynamic:** Amara is dismissed as a relic, fueling her self-doubt. When Victoria acts rash and endangers the group, Amara gets irate, seeing this trait in herslf.

   
 

**3. Signs of Trouble** 

- Psykers around the convent go mysteriously missing. 
- **Tensions:** Victoria undermines Amara during training exercises. Amara becomes irate with Victoria after her arrogance almost kills someone. Amara suspects cracks in Castella’s leadership but hesitates to act due to lack of confidence, "What if I'm wrong?". 


 **4. Chaos Invasion and Castella’s Betrayal** 

- Chaos forces led by Arcanus attack Veridia, overwhelming the Imperial defenses. 
- Reveal: Castella orchestrated the invasion, killing psykers to open a Warp portal. 
- **Climactic Moment:** Castella nearly kills Victoria, but Amara intervenes and kills her in a desperate, brutal battle. 

   
 

**5. Ignatia Takes Command** 

- With Castella dead, the squad is left leaderless. Amara steps up, despite resistance and doubts. Issues a vox broadcast to all surviving sisters, to recover and regroup at the underground bunker.
- **Symbolism:** Her malfunctioning rosarius reflects her wavering confidence. 
- **Discovery:** Chaos forces plan to open a 'backdoor' Warp gate on Veridia which can be used to launch attacks across Imperial worlds. Amara recalls war stories about Arcanus being nearly immune to conventional fire. 

   

## **Act 2: Rising Action and All-is-Lost** 


**6. Rallying the Squad** 

- Amara confronts Victoria’s insubordination, earning grudging respect by demonstrating her strategic brilliance. 
- Amara solidifies her role as leader, despite lingering doubts in her team and herself. 

   
 

**7. The Mission** 

- Amara uncovers Vox-records revealing Arcanus’s involvement and the ritual’s location. 
- **Objective:** Assassinate Arcanus to prevent the gate from opening fully. 
- **Roles Assigned:** 

- **Victoria:** Leads the assault. 
- **Justine:** technological countermeasures and vehicle expert. 
- **Silva:** Medical expert and supplies manager. 

   
 

**8. The Colony** 

- The squad discovers survivors at a mining colony and an ecclesiarch temple. 
- **Conflict:** They encounter corrupted Sisters, forced to kill them after uncovering their heresy. 
- **Emotional Weight:** Survivors struggle with the implications of this betrayal. 

   
 

**9. The Shrine** 

- Silva stabilizes wounded colonists, performing a small divine miracle. 
- **Supplies Gained:** Warp-altered krak grenade from Chaos Sisters’ equipment. 
- **Vision:** Amara sees an abandoned cathedral linked to the ritual but keeps it to herself. 

   
 

**10. The Factory** 


- The squad finds a damaged Castigator tank in a nearby factory. Justine leads efforts to repair it. 
- **The Lie**- Her hopelessness and lack of confidence are at odds with **The Truth**- the hope she has, her battle genius and her belief in her redemption. This scene is where she ultimatley must let go of her lie and choose the truth. There can be no victory without hope and confidence in herself.

- **Daemon Attack:** The group is ambushed and separated shortly after entering the factory. Silva fights effectively for the first time, reconciling her combat role with her healing duties. 

- **Amara and Victoria** are forced into the upper levels of the factory, where unstable catwalks and rusted machinery create dangerous terrain.
- **Victoria’s Doubts:** She begins by challenging Amara’s strategy, blaming her for leading them into a trap. 
- **Amara’s  Doubts:** Amara is at odds with herself, this insecurity must be reflected internally. She made a bad decision to trap the team in the factory and now she feels this weight. Victoria and Amara will have a moment of connection- a moment of approval, this is when Amara knows she has to let go of **The Lie**. 
- **Victoria’s Growth:** At a critical moment, Victoria risks herself to hold off a daemonette, saving Amara. Display Amara's strategy in this situation in tandym with Victoria's youthful athleticism that they use to defeat the enemy. 

- **Silva and Justine** are trapped near the tank, with daemons attempting to breach their position. 

- **Justine’s Brilliance:** Justine uses her ingenuity, modifying a broken servo-arm to jury-rig an improvised defense system around the tank. 
- **Silva’s Courage:** Silva overcomes her reluctance to fight, wielding her boltgun effectively to protect Justine as she works. 
- **Dynamic Growth:** Justine’s chatter and cheeriness lighten Silva’s nerves, helping her find balance between healer and warrior. 

- Amara and Victoria rejoin Silva and Justine just as the psyker’s Warp barrier collapses. Together, they destroy the remaining Chaos forces, including the psyker. 
- **The Castigator Tank as a Symbol:** Justine finishes repairing the tank in time to use it to clear the final wave, marking their first major triumph as a team. 
- Amara finds some solace in the improved team work and her leadership despite her earlier doubts.   



**11. Campfire** 

- **Bonding Moment:** The squad shares personal stories and desires: 

- Amara: Redemption and proving herself as a leader. 
- Victoria: Fear of being forgotten. 
- Justine: Frustration with being undervalued. 
- Silva: Snaps at the group, citing her inability to save civilians at the Shrine. Struggles with her destiny as a destroyer. 

- **Plan:** Amara asks Justine to modify the krak grenade using Warp-touched materials, sparking moral tension. 

   
 

**Act 3: Climax and Falling Action** 

**12. Discovering the Ritual Site** 

- The squad deciphers clues from Vox-records, Chaos relics, leading them to the abandoned cathedral. 

   
 

**13. Through Enemy Territory** 

- The squad navigates Chaos-controlled areas, and rendevous with some guardsman
- **Amara’s Leadership:** The squad begins to see her as the legendary figure from her stories. 

   
 

**14. Confronting Arcanus** 

- **The Battle:** 

- They arrive too late, Arcanus has already opened up the backdoor, signaling the beginning of the end.
- Justine provides cover fire with the Castigator tank. 
- Victoria and Silva engage Arcanus directly, drawing his attention. 
- Amara confronts Arcanus in a climactic duel. 

- **Psychological Struggle:** Amara sees herself as a fragile old woman but uses memories of past victories to reignite her strength. It is at this moment, she fully relinquishes the lie and becomes the warrior she always has been; the old Amara dies and the new is all that remains, one that lives that truth.
- **Victory:** Amara's previously malfunctioning rosarius miraculously protects her from Arcanus' superior attacks, giving her enough time to detonate the Warp-altered grenade, killing Arcanus but suffering mortal wounds herself. The 'backdoor' is secured. 
- This battle in many ways needs to serve as the 'After' in the 'Before/After'. This will show the reader how far Amara has come. In her internal battle of hope and confidence.

   
 

**15. Amara’s Sacrifice** 

- Victoria cradles Amara as she dies, receiving her rosarius and symbolic leadership. 
- Silva and Justine kneel by her side. 
-  It is now that we see Amara's blood that soaks the snow, uncannily crawls through the runes of the site, extinguishing the energy and closing the warp. 
- Reinforcements arrive, Imperial Guard and Sisters of Battle fighting together to secure the site and protect it.
