Amara is considering suicide in her dormitory while the other sisters are tending to the convent. She holds the gun to her head but doesn't do anything. Too tired or maybe even too ashamed. It has been roughly 10 years now she's been stationed on this planet. She survived numerous battles as a Sister Repentia, even the one's were death was certain. The Ecclesiarchy, not knowing how to handle her, decided to send her to help establish a convent and offer protections to miners.

This planet, called Valon Minor, was once a thriving mining world, it had the potential to become a hive world or maybe even an agri world. But, for a reason lost to time, the people died off or left. Rediscovered only recently, the Ecclesiarchy ordered that the world be used for whatever minuscule mining for resources could be done and to use it as a training center for sisters seen as too undesirable. weather it be their past failures, infractions or simply because you pissed off the wrong person, no one wants to be here.

The major characters are introduced as Amara trains them in the way of war. Some of them, primarily Victoria, dismisses her as a relic. During an exercise, Victoria endangers some of her sisters, almost getting them killed which infuriates Amara, reminding her of her former self.

The room was dim and the air was still. She was used to it at this point, but it was never pleasant. The biting cold seemed to pierce the stone despite how thick it was, and turned the dormitory into a refrigerator. The sisters tended to hand purity seals up on the walls to help insulate whatever heat they could.

She was up earlier than everyone else, but soon the lights would come on and they would find her. In her hand was a bolt pistol, standard issue for the Adepta Sororitas. It was heavy piece of iron but nothing fancy was necessary. She figured since no foe she faced in her career was able to take her out, the job fell to her. 

*No foe could match me, and I am unable to earn a warrior's death. The Emperor has abandoned me and left me here. Its okay- just let go.* 

The cracked, jittered voice of a cherub broke the silence, "Sister Amara, you are requested at the reliquary." It rounded the corner into her dormitory and she quickly concealed the weapon under her fleece blanket.

"Sister Superior Castella commands it. Please awake early to beat the new arrivals there."

Amara rose and began to don her armor, "At her command. You may leave, homunculus."

There was a harsh buzz from the window's mechanism before the metal shutter opened. The light poured in and was a sharp contrast to her eyes- briefly blinding her. The window revealed a yellow sunrise blanketing the sheet-white planet, Valon Minor. 

The reliquary was lit only by candles and the natural light that seeped through the stained glass windows. The design upon the glass was that of Saint Celestine, with her sword raised above her head, as though it were holding up the roof.

Amara turned to the Shrine at the front of the room, tended to the candles, and prayed.

*Grand Emperor of Light and Order, though I don't deserve it, I ask thou for another day. Another day to train the new sisters who may soon take my place. Allow them the courage to stand where I fall. If thou wills it, I will walk another day.*

She became lost in thought and prayer, muttering to herself with her old, inert rosarius tangled in her hands. Several pairs of footsteps clanked behind her, the new arrivals. They knew better than to speak during a prayer, so they knelt and waited. 

Eventually the elder opened her eyes and faced them, beckoning them to rise.
"Sisters, welcome. I am Amara Ignatia, formerly of the Argent Shroud," she hiked her boltgun to her shoulder and led the girls outside. One of the girls glared at Amara and furrowed her brow. 

"If you're here, then the Ecclesiarchy has deemed you troublesome and in need of specialized training." 

*Why Castella and the Ecclesiarchy find me to to the best choice escapes me.*

When the heavy stone doors opened to the outside, they were met with the intensity of the planet's environment. 

They made her way out to the field in the back where training equipment had been set up. There were several different training courses for different skills ranging from raw athleticism obstacle courses to firing ranges for all types of weapons. For miles there was nothing but a blank wasteland but deep in the distance, one could scarcely make out mountains that spiked up from the horizon.

The three silver figures following Amara marched to position, side by side. She eyed them up and down for a while before she worked up the energy to be a hard-ass, "Names."

The one furthest left spoke up first, "Justine Gladius, ma'am."

Justine pulled her goggles from her face and placed them on her forehead, sweeping her bangs upwards. Her skin was darker than Amara's, but she still couldn't make out her face very well. A rebreather, typical of the tech priests covered her nose and mouth.

"Your face, why are you hiding it?" 

"Greatest apologies, sister. I was born on the forge-world of Mars, it brings comfort to my lungs. The fumes from the great furnaces have taken their toll upon me. May I continue to wear it?"

Amara shook annoyance from her voice, "Very well, if it will allow you to perform better."
