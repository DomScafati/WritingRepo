## Maddax notes:
The “fear of the dark” layer makes Maddax much more than a stock villain. 

It turns him into a **tragic existential antagonist**—a character who’s violent not because he enjoys chaos, but because it’s the only way he can prove to himself (and others) that he’s _still here_.

Think the way _Blade Runner_ replicants cling to memories and sensory experiences because they know they’ll eventually be erased

. In Maddax’s case, it’s even worse—he’s _already been erased once_.

That lets you explore questions like:

- Was his “soul” actually pulled from oblivion, or is he just a perfect simulation with a convincing illusion of past consciousness?
    
- If Daedalus actually snatched him out of the afterlife (or nothingness), does that make them gods, devils, or grave robbers?
    
- If he _is_ the original Maddax, what does that say about the permanence of death in your setting?
    

The saltwater docks memory you mentioned is perfect—those small, mundane moments are often what haunt people most when they think of what they’ll lose.  
I could imagine him lashing out with something like:

> “You don’t know what it’s like.  
> There’s nothing.  
> No dreams. No color. No sound.  
> One moment I was breathing in the docks at night, the salt air sticking to my teeth—  
> the next, it was all gone.  
> No ‘me.’ Just _void_.  
> Then I woke up _here_.  
> And every day I can feel it waiting to take me back.”

If we keep this angle, his early protectiveness toward Mahoney could work because:

1. She’s proof he’s still connected to the physical world.
    
2. She may be one of the few who understands how the EE works—meaning she could be his only hope to prevent “going dark” again.
    

Later, once desperation sets in, that protectiveness could shift into coercion or outright hostility—because to him, keeping her close may become a matter of survival, even if it means forcing her.

Maddax was the son of a fisherman on Earth, idolizing his father’s skill and joy in life. When a bombing run killed his father, leaving him orphaned, Maddax became obsessed with avenging him. He joined the military, channeling his grief into becoming a lethal, sophisticated operative, excelling at wetwork. His skill and cold efficiency made him highly sought after, but when his side lost the war, he was captured, executed, and entombed on Io. Daedalus later recovered his body, scanning his neural pathways into the Epitaph Engine — dragging him back from the nothingness he fears above all else.

## **Behavioral Tics**

- **Fisherman’s Habits:** Uses sea metaphors (“the tide’s turning,” “hooked clean through”), hums shanties under his breath, and sharpens blades like mending nets.
    
- **Predatory Patience:** Comfortable in silence, observing, waiting for the perfect moment to act.
    
- **Tool Care Rituals:** Handles weapons with meticulous precision, treating them like irreplaceable tools from a fishing boat.
    
- **Crowd Scanning:** Always assessing exits, choke points, and faces — like reading the water for signs of a catch.
