# **Guidelines for Story Beats**

The Ghost: Heather's ghost is that her husband died from disease back on her homeworld. This sent her into a spiral of apathy and depression. She no longer cares what happens to her.

The Lie: Heather is a shadow to her own life with no agency, therefore, her lie is that she doesn't believe she's good enough to make a change in the world. Apathy.

The Truth: Heather learns that she is good enough, therefore, the truth is that she is good enough to make a change in the world. Passion- apathy inverted.

## **Setting:** The DSS Tubman
In the year 3030, the Deep Space Station Tubman stands above all it's contemporaries as a marvel of modern engineering. It is a 30 billion dollar space station deployed in the deepest regions of space for the purpose of neurological research and development. The government contracted the architectural development to Daedalus Dynamics, a mega conglomerate with secret ulterior motives. The station was purposefully designed with several decks filled with looping corridors and labyrinthian hallways.

A crew of several dozen government scientists, engineers, security, and habitation specialists work together to make the mission of developing a drug that will make individuals with extreme anti-social personality disorder a functioning member of society.

However, Daedalus has implanted the personality of a highly trained, previously executed killer into one of the crew using a device called The Epitaph Engine. The assassin's mission is simple, kill 2 targets, and steal the research, do so will as little damage to the station as possible.

Note: this is also the general chronological order. 

**Purpose:** Establish normalcy, drip-feed unease, foreshadow Maddax without giving him full presence.
- Story is told through compiled logs and transcripts
- First entry should be a company memorandum that is highly redacted but gives hints to what the story will be about
- Have logs of daily life, building characters and learning about the station.
- Mahoney's logs give very light hints that something is wrong with her, like spotting a strange man or losing time
- Zan is killed by Maddax

- ends with spooky and cryptic log of maddax

## **Act 2 – The Breakdown and Approaching of the Truth**

**Purpose:** Escalate from mystery to paranoia, fracture crew trust, expose Gloriya, Heather is beginning to believe in her "Truth", yet still rejects it until the end of the act.
Note: punish heather when she strays from the "Truth" towards the "Lie"

- Leadership struggles to find who is doing this and why
- To get to his target, Maddax decides to set up a timed-release on his target prisoner's cryo-pod. But is captured by the crew before he can get to him. Because of this, the prisoner lets the other prisoners go free.
- Heather is interrogated while the crew gathers and watches.
- Gloriya and Axton want due process and crew wants to throw her out the airlock.
- While the whole crew is distracted he prisoners, hyper violent, decide to kill off the crew and plan on ransoming the station.
- Axton has to maintain ship's course in the chaos
- Several days of fighting ensue before almost everyone is dead
- Heather stops Maddax from killing Axton, but he's critically wounded.
- This is final straw for heather and she is determined to get her life back.

## **Act 3 – The Rejection of the Lie and Embrace of the Truth**

**Purpose:** Show Mahoney reclaiming control, tie together distrust, corporate cruelty, and Maddax’s threat.

- The heart of this act is that Heather will be embracing her "Truth" fully and rejecting the "Lie".
- Heather has to defend her "truth" from maddax's insistence on the "lie".
- Maddax and Mahoney fight for her soul ideologically and somewhat physically.
- Heather kills Maddax by self mutilation and destroying the epitaph engine
- Ends with AI memorandum summarizing the events and reporting on the recovery team.
- Most of the awake crew is dead except for Heather and Axton, cryo sleep crew is generally fine.
