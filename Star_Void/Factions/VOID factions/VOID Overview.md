**Venerated Order of Imperial Dominion**  
_(Commonly known as VOID)_

---

## Overview

Once part of the [[STAR Overview|Sovereign Territories and Allied Republics]], the **Venerated Order of Imperial Dominion** served as a loyal—if zealous—arm of the empire for centuries. That changed in **2101**, when the Order declared independence under the rule of [[The King in Yellow]], a living avatar of [[They]] who survived multiple injections of **They Brain Matter**. His ascension marked the birth of a new theocratic state, unified by faith, fear, and resentment.

VOID’s founding worlds were among the harshest in the galaxy—planets ravaged by corporate exploitation, extreme climates, and failed terraforming projects. Cut off from STAR’s protection and oversight, these systems evolved into brutal theocracies that weaponized **Gene Debt**, **Slavery**, and **Marrow worship** as tools of control. The people who once suffered beneath corporate dominion turned those same methods into their creed.

---

## Culture and Religion

Unlike the secular faiths of STAR, VOID rejects [[The Great Spirit]] entirely.  
Their central belief holds that the Spirit was **killed at the dawn of time** by [[They]]—primordial entities that exist beyond the Fifth Dimension. To the faithful, **They** are not demons but **gods of strength, violence, and transcendence**.

VOID’s worship is not prayer, but **submission through mutation**. Followers willingly inject **Marrow** or even **Brain Matter**, believing that each graft brings them closer to divine perfection. The faithful see the body as clay—an unfinished vessel to be sculpted by They’s will.

> “To suffer mutation is to be seen.  
> To die from it is to be chosen.”

---

## Society

VOID society mirrors its theology: hierarchical, cruel, and absolute. Each system is ruled by a **High Synod**, answerable only to the King in Yellow. The economy runs on **flesh and labor**—citizens are born into **Gene Debt**, and redemption often means servitude or transformation.

Technology is primitive compared to STAR’s scientific dominance. VOID’s advancements focus instead on **bio-alchemy**, **resonant grafting**, and **ritualized warfare**. Innovation serves faith, not progress.

---

## Species of VOID

- **[[Caprikon]]** – The spiritual core of VOID. Eldritch goatmen whose natural **high resonance** makes them ideal priests, prophets, and executioners of the faith.
    
- **[[Nyctari]]** – Towering moth-like warriors and tacticians. Their cunning and ferocity make them natural conquerors and enforcers.
    
- **Humans** – The most populous species, valued for their adaptability. Though **inert to resonance**, they use Marrow more consistently than any other race—at great personal risk. Their flesh is both weapon and offering.
    

---

## Military and Power

VOID’s armies are vast—**outnumbering STAR three to one**—but lack its precision and discipline. Where STAR fights with machine logic, VOID fights with religious fervor. Their ranks are swollen with **Marrow soldiers**, **mutants**, and **bio-forged monstrosities** whose very existence defies physics.

The King in Yellow’s followers believe victory is preordained; death only marks a soldier’s ascension into They’s eternal choir.

---

## Ethos

- **Faith is Flesh:** The body is the temple and the weapon.
- **Pain is Proof:** Suffering affirms devotion.
- **Resonance is Truth:** To hear They is to live without fear.

## Inspiration / Aesthetic

**Influences:** Warhammer Chaos, Mad Max tribes, medieval theocracy, Bloodborne, cosmic horror.  
**Visual Motifs:** Tarnished gold and bone-white armor, ritual scars, fungal growths, candles melted into armor plates, banners of flayed hide.  
**Audio/Mood Cues:** Gregorian chant mixed with distorted radio static; the hum of living engines; whispers of unseen gods.
