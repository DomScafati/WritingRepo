# **Species of the Known Galaxy**

## **Common Species**

### **Humans**

_Counterpart:_ Imperial Guard / Standard Fantasy Humans  
The most populous species across the galaxy. Divided by ideology more than biology, their societies range from democratic republics to monarchic empires. Northern humans view **Marrow** as heresy; southern humans embrace it as divine evolution.

---

### **Jackalopes**

_Counterpart:_ Hobbits / Folk Heroes  
Whimsical horned rabbit-kin known for mischief, hospitality, and a stubborn defense of their homelands. Though often underestimated, they possess surprising agility and supernatural instincts for stealth and survival.

---

### **Hidefolk**

_Counterpart:_ Wood Elves / Ents  
Nomadic woodland giants with immense strength and wisdom. Deeply spiritual, guided by **Grand Shamans** who commune with nature spirits. Rarely engage in war unless provoked or their forests are desecrated.

---

### **Mothmen

_Counterpart:_ Uruk-hai / Dark Elves  
Violent moth- and butterfly-like hominids who perceive time as a flat circle. Their minds operate across overlapping timelines, making them terrifying opponents. Once scholars of light, now fallen into fanaticism.

---

### **Star People**

_Counterpart:_ Celestials / Devils  
Short, green, psionically gifted beings from a hidden pocket dimension. They communicate only through telepathy and manipulate energy at a level bordering on divine. Their motives remain inscrutable — saviors or saboteurs depending on who you ask.

---

### **Caprikon**

_Counterpart:_ Orks / Goblins  
Goat-kin zealots who serve **They**, the sleeping gods of the dark cosmos. Fearless, joyless, and devoted entirely to spreading their masters’ influence through sacrifice and corruption.

---

### **Skinwalkers (Clones)**

_Counterpart:_ Drow / Drukhari  
Originally human colonists who turned to cloning for survival. Centuries of replication produced degenerative flaws — physical, mental, and moral. Fragmented into raiding clans, they terrorize the fringes of space.  
Known variations of primary template:

- **Warrior:** Brutal frontliners
    
- **Rogue:** Strategic infiltrators
    
- **Marrower:** High tolerance for alien grafts, extremely rare
    

---

## **Uncommon & Monstrous Species**

### **Thunderbirds**

_Counterpart:_ Dragons  
Colossal avian creatures adapted to any climate or atmosphere. Their feathers generate bioelectric storms capable of powering entire cities — or annihilating them.

---

### **Wendigos**

_Counterpart:_ Trolls  
Twisted Jackalopes corrupted by flesh-hunger and dark resonance. Their transformation is irreversible — they exist only to feed and to howl beneath alien moons.

---

### **They**

_Counterpart:_ C’tan / Lovecraftian Old Gods  
Primordial entities from before the stars formed. Their decayed corpses are harvested for **Marrow**, **Bone**, and **Brain Matter** — materials that grant mortals terrifying power at catastrophic cost.

---

## ** Other **

### **Rangers**

_Alignment:_ Neutral Good  
Elite frontiersmen and monster-hunters infused with controlled Marrow grafts. They operate beyond government oversight, mapping uncharted systems, recovering alien artifacts, and exterminating hostile fauna.  
Note: typically (but not limited to) human, because of the human Z gene that allows for less Marrow rejection. Also, the Orders were started on human colonized worlds, so non-humans would be the exception, not the rule.


---

## **Political Geography**

### **Northern Coalition (The “Good” Factions)**

- **Humans (LG):** Democratic; Marrow is taboo
- **Jackalopes (CG):** Rural peacekeepers, quick to defend
- **Sasquatch (NG):** Mystical nomads, nature-bound

---

### **Southern Dominion (The “Evil” Factions)**

- **Humans (LE):** Ruled by a monarch who survived multiple Brain Matter injections, becoming a divine avatar
- **Dunwich (NE):** Religious extremists devoted to They
- **Mothmen (CE):** Inhuman warriors who blend magic, flight, and terror

---

### Wild Cards
- **Rangers (NG):** Freelance protectors and explorers
- **Star People (NG):** Rarely seen, psionic allies
- **Clone Raiders (CN):** pirate scavengers
- **Corporations (TN):** Amoral profit engines, these are corporate empires that own planets, shipping lanes, and citizens.
	- **Rockateers:** Interstellar rail and cargo monopoly
    - **Expansions Inc.:** Frontier colonization and indentured servitude
	- **Black Shock:** Marrow research and bio-weapons development
- **They (LE):** Dormant but ever-looming influence
