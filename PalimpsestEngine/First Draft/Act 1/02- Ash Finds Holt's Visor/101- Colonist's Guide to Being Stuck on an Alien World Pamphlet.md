So, your colony is trapped on an Alien planet. Tough luck, eh?

Have no fear, at OpenArtificial, our goal is to make your stay as manageable as possible. If you're reading this, your captain may have landed on the wrong planet or stranded you too far from a corporate designated rendevous point. As a Crawler, it is your job to ensure the survival of all the colonists in cryosleep. 

This pamphlet will feature a quick overview on the 4 tenets of staying healthy and positive: CARE.

[Image of a non-descript planet]

C - Caution
This new world will have any variety of weather, from sulfuric acid hurricanes (ouch!) to  sunny skies. Atmosphere, that could be human breathable or a complete vacuum. Even the flora can be edible just as easily as it can be carnivorous!

Some planets will be perfect models just like Old Earth, but this is certainly the exception, not the rule. 

Always wear your EVA suit when venturing into the unknown wilds!

A - Adaptibility
The second step to stay creative and open to new ideas! 

While on this alien planet, you may find that you must fight for resources with natives or even eachother! 

Food:
While we ate OpenArtificial never support unneccsary cannibalism, we do offer fiscal incentives for Crawler who are able to do whatever it takes to complete their mission!

Air:
If your oxygen is running low, try meditative exercises to lower your heart rate and oxygen consumption. Those who are always relaxed, always come back!

Water:
Your Deep Space Seed vessel should have a built-in water recycler, but if it has been damaged or cannot keep up with colony yield, you may have to reuse body water (icky!) but desperate times call for desperate measures!

R - Recovery
Remember to search for ship parts that may have come detached from the vessel during stranding, these can be reused and recycled in the Engineer's lab*.

As with other outside excursions, always wear your company designated EVA suit. The EVA suit  is equiped with pneumatic propulsion systems for rapid arial movement as well as advanced life support systems. These life support systems include, but are not limited to, 12 hours of oxygen, homeostasis regulators, and nutrition paste applicators. 

*Only Crawlers with an Engineer's pass may directly participate in adjustments and repairs to the ship.

E - Exodus

After a long period of vessel stranding, you may feel lost, but trust in the process. If you and your fellow Crawlers followed the previous steps, you will be a primed and ready for the final and most important step: Exodus!

Let any feelings of stress and anger melt away as your blast back into the atmosphere of the planet. Remember to thank each other for the collaboration by submitting a survey to the ship's captain. The Crawler with the highest rankings will receive a pizza party for themselves and up to 4 collegues, sponsored by Lorbo's Pizza.

Now get out there and stalk, stalk, stalk!
