I heard slow, purposeful footsteps around the corner. I got behind the rusted metal machine that partially blockaded the entrance and aimed my weapon.

I called out to the shooter, "Who's there? You with the Ulysses colony?"

Turning the corner was a man, in a long fur coat. Hands lazily raised above his head in an annoyed "I surrender" fashion.

His cold weather gear seemed to be made from whatever creatures were running around this planet. It was a patchwork of large quills and thick bear-like fur and one of his shoulders had a made from the skull of a three-eyed beast. Long, dirty blond hair hung in tendrils over his face. 

He kicked the corpse of one of the mutants that laid in a visceral pile, "Does this answer your question?" I hea rd my own voice call back to me. It took me a several moments before I was able to decipher his face. My face. I was too stunned to speak, he looked exactly like If I had been one of those hermit survivalists you hear about on the frontier.

*What the actual fuck is going on here? Is that my twin? I don't have any goddamn siblings, at least not anymore.* 

His face showed no surprise, which confused me until I realized my perception visor was still on. Not only was my face covered, but the visor projects the wearer's voice in low-fidelity, altering the sound. He had no idea that we looked alike. I slowly lowered my rail revolver, too stunned to speak anything coherent. 

"Yeah thats-thanks for the assist. What are you doing here?"

He cocked his head to the side, examining me and lowering his hands. I could see he had his weapon slung over his shoulder. It was a scavenged railgun. Much worse condition than my own, though impressive in it's own right. The fact that he could make such a crude weapon function without blowing himself up was serendipitous.

"This is *my* zone, Crawler. Which are you? Holt, Martynn?"

I froze for a second. Contemplating my next words very carefully. I wrestled with myself, *Should I tell him? Take my mask off? No, for now lets play it close to the chest.* "Don't know 'em. I'm a colonist, you wouldn't know me. Got myself off the ark. Call me, er, Gillian."

He didn't say anything. He just stared at me for a moment before snapping, "Shut up, you're cruel, he's friendly, a colonist."

Who was this guy talking to? He did he have a radio or some sort of communicator? "What?" I replied, confused.
"Sorry he won't stop talking. But he makes a good point, your gear is a Crawlers, did you steal it?"

Trying to defuse any further questioning. "Yeah, had to. I needed it more than he did. Just grabbed the first bag I saw." He shrugged and beckoned me over.

"Zone is hot right now. More mutants and monsters soon. The shots and screaming gets them riled up. Come with me, we'll go to my camp and we can talk more, yeah? Follow close." He pointed to the east, towards one of the smaller monoliths at the edge of the city.

As we walked, he stayed low, but continued to break the silence with his own external dialogue. This guy, me, was out of his mind.



...

I examined anything I saw and made my way deeper. I crept slowly and steadily, being sure to make as little sound as possible. Thats when I saw it partially crushed under a stone, a skeleton. A *human* skeleton. 

I kneeled close and examined the crumpled remains. Whoever this was had similar attire to myself, A cold weather jacket and a bodysuit. The base of the skull had an I/O Jack and I ran my fingers over it. 

"Why did you leave?" I whispered, half to myself, half to the corpse. Then I grabbed my own jack, pulled lightly and felt the cord slide out. I snaked it over to the skeleton and plugged it in.
