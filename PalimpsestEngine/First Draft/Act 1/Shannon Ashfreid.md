I was on a beach.

It was completely silent except the waves crashing against nearby jagged, obsidian rocks. It smelled strongly of salt and seaweed, almost to the point of revulsion.

Sometimes when I looked out to sea, I could see people standing there. They were far away, but I could always tell who they were. I didn't know their names, but I never forgot the faces of the people I murdered. I never forgot their voices. Courage would occasionally swell up in my chest and I would walk out to face them, but I couldn't move through the water, it was like it was some other barrier I wasn't allowed to cross. I could yell at the faces, curse at them, but I never got a response, just a curious stare and a tiny, almost imperceptible sway.

One day, just beyond the rocks, I could see someone peaking around the corner at me. Someone small, maybe a child. They weren't that far but I couldn't see any features on them. While the child had my attention, I saw something heavy fall onto shore, just past the rock where I couldn't see.

When I made my way there, I saw a man who looked exactly like myself laying there. No, he was the same but different. I could see grown men crashing into the water, falling from the sky. But it was almost always past where I could go, further beyond than even the ghosts stood. Did they all those bodies look like me? 

I chose to sit next to him, the body on the shore, and he slowly started to get carried away by the current. The best thing I could do was just to let him go since I couldn't follow. Then his fingers twitched. They twitched.

I'd been trapped here for so long, with no movement, and this body just twitched. Before I could do anything he started to convulse violently like he was being electrocuted. I grabbed his hand and yanked him, back to the shore and he fell on me, screaming, shrieking. Then I woke up in the pod, screaming to myself. Punching the glass as hard as I can, fumbling with  it until I found an emergency release.
