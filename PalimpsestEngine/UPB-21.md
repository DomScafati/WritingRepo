UPB-21 is a small planet located several thousand light-years from the Sol system. It was once inhabited by hyper-intelligent beings whose technological development surpassed the limits of human comprehension. At the height of their advancement, they simply vanished. No records remain to explain where they went, why they left, or how their departure occurred. What they abandoned is known to human civilization only as **Unknown Planetary Body 021**, a designation reserved for worlds possessing a marginally hospitable atmosphere.

The planet is cold, averaging **15°F**, with temperatures dropping significantly lower near the poles. Atmospheric density is approximately **80% of Earth’s**, producing a range of abnormal effects. Sound does not travel far. Darkness spreads quickly. Light from the planet’s binary star system is harsh and high-contrast, distorting depth and shadow in ways that play tricks on the mind. Prolonged exposure often induces paranoia—weeks of flinching at shadows—followed by a dangerous sense of false safety. It is typically at this point that the planet’s predators strike.

Atmospheric composition further complicates survival. Elevated oxygen levels cause fires to ignite rapidly and unpredictably, while increased carbon dioxide creates a taxing aerobic environment, particularly for mammals. Even without direct injury, long-term exposure degrades stamina, judgment, and endurance.

The Precursors lived among vast fields of monolithic obelisks, stretching across the landscape in orderly rows for miles before abruptly ending. These structures are composed of a light-absorbing, matte-black stone capable of geometric reconfiguration. When properly powered, the material is able to repair itself, reshaping damaged surfaces as if responding to an unseen blueprint.

Rune-etched stone roads connect these regions, glowing faintly beneath the ice. Massive autonomous trains still travel along them, suspended inches above the surface. The trains carry cargo millennia old, their purpose long forgotten, and do not slow or stop for human presence. These settlements—if they can be called cities—are dense with alien machinery that remains active, poorly understood, and frequently hazardous to human explorers.

Beyond the ruins lie dense forests that provide shelter from the planet’s relentless winds. Their canopies trap falling snow, creating zones of relative calm. These trees are heavily wooded but infested with chitinous insects bearing obsidian-black carapaces that emit a faint, ominous glow. The forests host flora and fauna pushed to the extreme end of evolutionary optimization. Any single species would qualify as an apex predator on Earth.

Many animals bear unsettling similarities to terrestrial life, though they are far better adapted to UPB-21’s harsher conditions. Plant life has evolved even more aggressive countermeasures, with numerous species developing carnivorous traits. The wilderness offers protection—but never safety.
