# 🔹 **THE LIE–TRUTH STORY TEMPLATE

_A Character-Driven, Theme-First Narrative Checklist_

---

## CORE STORY DNA

### **Theme**


- Survival alone is not humane; humanity is what makes survival meaningful.


---

### **The Truth**

- I am responsible for what happens when I choose not to act.
- Humanity requires engagement, not detachment.


---

### **The Lie**

- Survival is the only virtue that matters.
- Apathy is protection


---

### **The Ghost**

- Was unable to assist a passenger ship from being destroyed, so he convinces himself that the fact he didn't intervene was the reason he survived and therefore the best outcome.

---

## PROTAGONIST DEFINITION

_(Ensure the character embodies the theme)_

---

### **Protagonist at the Start**

> Who is this person because of the Lie?

**Checklist:**

- ☐ Competent and functional
- ☐ Emotionally guarded
- ☐ The Lie appears to work
- ☐ Avoids responsibility framed as “realism” or “professionalism”

**Guiding Question:**

> _How does this character survive — and what does it cost them?_

---

### **Protagonist at the End**

> Who are they after confronting the Truth?

**Checklist:**

- ☐ Makes an active, costly choice
- ☐ Accepts consequences without deflection
- ☐ Acts differently under similar pressure
- ☐ Embodies the theme through behavior



## STORY STRUCTURE (ACT-BY-ACT)

## **ACT I — NORMAL WORLD & DISRUPTION**

### **Normal World**

> Show the Lie in action.

Include:

- ☐ Routine shaped by the Lie
- ☐ Subtle dissatisfaction or hollowness
- ☐ Hints of the Ghost
- ☐ Truth framed as naïve, dangerous, or impractical


---

### **Call to Action**

> The event that forces engagement with the story’s moral problem.

Requirements:

- ☐ Challenges the Lie directly
- ☐ Cannot be ignored without consequence
- ☐ Introduces moral stakes, not just danger

**Test:**

> _If the protagonist refuses, does that refusal say something about who they are?_

---

### **Refusal / Hesitation**

(Optional but powerful)

- Protagonist uses the Lie to justify delay or distance.
- This reinforces the Lie’s internal logic.


## **ACT II — PRESSURE & CONSEQUENCES**

---

### **Rising Action**

> The world increasingly punishes adherence to the Lie.

Checklist:

- ☐ Truth appears repeatedly in different forms
- ☐ Protagonist partially understands but resists
- ☐ Temporary fixes fail
- ☐ Secondary characters embody extremes of Lie or Truth

---

### **High Cost for Truth Aversion**

> The price of _not_ changing becomes unavoidable.

Types of cost:

- ☐ Moral complicity
- ☐ Innocent harm
- ☐ Lost opportunity
- ☐ Escalating danger
- ☐ Emotional numbness or corrosion

**Key Question:**

> _Does clinging to the Lie now cause active harm?_

---

### **Midpoint (Optional)**

- Partial insight.
- Protagonist believes they’ve “solved” the problem without changing fully.

---

## **ACT III — DECISION & RECKONING**

---

### **Collapse of the Lie**

> The Lie can no longer function.

- Survival alone is insufficient.
- The Ghost resurfaces (explicitly or emotionally).

---

### **The Choice (CLIMAX)**

> A decision between Lie and Truth.

Requirements:

- ☐ No neutral option
- ☐ No guaranteed success
- ☐ Choice defines identity

**Reminder:**

> The climax is a _moral decision_, expressed through action.

---

### **16. Resolution**

> Show the consequences.

Checklist:

- ☐ World reflects the choice
- ☐ Theme is proven, not explained
- ☐ Ending feels earned and inevitable

---

## FINAL INTEGRITY CHECK

Before drafting, confirm:

- ☐ Theme = Truth generalized
- ☐ Lie explains early behavior
- ☐ Ghost explains Lie
- ☐ Plot pressures belief, not just body
- ☐ Avoiding the Truth worsens outcomes
- ☐ Ending answers the thematic question
