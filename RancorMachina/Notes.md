## Plot

#### Setting:
Years ago the Arbiters, specially trained mech pilots, fought back subterranean monsters and destroyed their queen, saving the world from a terrifying threat. However, before they had any time to savor their victory they faced a new, much more lethal threat. Now humanity is a planet on the brink of extinction from two AI controlled mechs called The Twins. They emerge unpredictably from their hiding to commit genocide against humanity. After twenty years of this, humanity is morally and physically broken down. Every time humans begin to rebuild, The Twins show up to destroy them. 

#### Act 1: setup, normal world and call-to-action
- Grace is training Renata to be the next generation of Arbiters, mech pilots who defend humanity from monsters. Their training consists of hunting relatively small but dangerous subterranean monsters.
- Renata is not a good pilot at all and she struggles with this despite Grace's reassuring words.
- We learn Grace is the last Arbiter, a conclave of pilots specializing in mech combat who defended the world from monsters. They were all killed by The Twins.
- The two get a radio alert that The Twins are attacking a nearby city.
- Seeing Renata isn't ready to fight, Grace disables her mech and goes to fight The Twins alone.
- Grace saves several hundred people, but is killed fighting Rancor and Entropy.
- Renata finally catches up, seeing Grace is dead, she tries to fight The Twins too, but she is almost killed, herself.
- Renata crawls inside of Grace's cockpit with her corpse and cries.
- Renata begins to believe the lies: She is innadequate because of her human error. This internalizes as suvivors guilt, anger, inadequacy and obsessive training.
- Renata vows revenge against The AI menace.

#### Act 2: introduction to the truth, all-is-lost breakup arch, and rising action
- Renata obsessively trains, affirming in herself the lie
- Renata's brother, Zechariah is a mechanic and helps her train. This includes hunting smaller monsters together that attack their settlement.
- They argue and bicker, this is where we her flaws.
- Zech eventually helps her see that it was not her fault. He begins to show her the truth.
- She initially rejects the idea of the truth and trains harder, becoming psychologically intertwined with her mech. She is believing the lie and become less human, becoming mean and cold to her brother.
- She rewatches recordings of the fight and figures out that the The Twins were hacking into her mech and gathering vital information, giving them edges in the fight. This included Ammo Count, audio transcriptions from the cockpit, system inputs, etc. They were even able to stall vital functions for piloting, making her movements more sluggish.
- She guts her mech of computer systems, making it fully manual. Something Grace did, but was very challenging.
- She apologizes to Zech and he helps her train again, becoming a vital assett as she begins to believe the truth. She begins to accept that it wasn't her fault and she's lucky to be alive. Additionally, becoming colder is not the same as becoming stronger.

#### Act 3: climax, affirmation of the truth, and resolution
- Renata faces off against The Twins when they emerge again.
- She is a different person, but they are exactly the same. This is where she affirms her truth. against them.
- The Twins are a dark mirror to renata. Internally renata risked becoming cold and inhuman through her trauma and obsession. Externally, she began to integrate too closely to her machine. Inversely, the Twins represent a visage of what she could have become.
- She battles both of them, killing Entropy first.
- Almost dies to Rancor, but as her mech is destroyed, she is able to jump into his cockpit.
- She sees that he isn't an AI, he is a man with no arms or legs, directly integrated into the mech. The symbol on his gear indicates that he was the leader of the Arbiters.
- She doesn't win despite being human, she wins because she is human.
## Renata Honeybone's Positive Change arc
#### The Lie
- Renata believes the lie that she is inadequate and that she could have killed the machines if she was stronger and angrier
- Renata believes that by becoming more intertwined with her machine and removing the human error, she will be stronger
  
#### The Truth
- Renata learns the truth that what happened was simply out of her control and she's lucky to be alive. Careful preparation and planning is what she needs.
- Throwing away her humanity and removing the human element doesn't make her better than the machines, it simply makes her less human.

#### The Ghost
- Renata is haunted by the fact that Grace Sonnenchein died and she couldn't stop it
