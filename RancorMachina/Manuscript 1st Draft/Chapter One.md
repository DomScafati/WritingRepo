The massive steel heels dug into the dirt as she took a runner's stance, "Alright, you ready."
"Won't know until we try."
"That-a-girl."
In an explosive instant, Grace's mech burst forward. Each stomp rumbled the earth beneath her and startled birds from the tree line. The goal was simple, take the flag off the back of Renata's mech. It would teach her the defensive skills necessary of an Arbiter, like space control, footwork, and tracking. Grace was working with 20 years of experience and had worked out a comprehensive plan to train this new generation, starting with her pupil here. She was in a good mood, so she decided to take it easy on her.

The child awkwardly held her hands up defensively and stumbled back, shocked at the speed of Grace's *Kiloton*. Several quick punches burst from the mentor's titanic arms, just barely being blocked. Grace noticed she was focused on keeping her head and torso aligned too much and wasn't watching her steps. The mentor noticed immediately and pressed this weakness with sweeping, heavy strikes. Slow enough to react to, but intimidating to the novice and sending her several yards back. 

The plan was simple, condition her opponent with a flurry of strikes, set up an obvious haymaker, and when Renata gets ready to block, Grace would feint and snatch the flag.

To Grace's surprise, as she readied her winning blow, the girl kicked up a mound of dirt obscuring her before closing the distance. The *Kiloton* was lean for a mech, but boasted long limbs for the pilot's preferred melee-oriented combat style. The trainee on the other hand had a shorter, stockier frame that could take more punishment. That lower center of gravity was a boon here. Grace couldn't allow herself to be tackled. As per the agreement, a knockdown would result in her loss. 

The hand of the *Kiloton* reached out, blocking the 'eyes' of the training mech and, with a swift knee to the torso, sent it stumbling back.

"Shit!" Renata said, realizing she had just been pushed over a fallen sequoia tree. Dust ploomed around where she landed, and with the precision of a cobra, the *Kiloton* snatched the flag.

"48 Seconds, Ren. Thats a PR."

Grace reached her hand out and helped the girl back on her feet. The opportunity to practice like this hadn't arisen in years and the middle aged woman grinned with amusement. Renata, however wasn't smiling.
"You made me look like a goddamn pushover. I even tried a dirty trick and still lost."
"Dirty Trick?" Grace was confused at her pupils distress, "You didn't play dirty, you were evening the odds. I've got several decades of experience to your two months." She powered off her mech and opened the cockpit and Renata did the same. They stood only a couple yards apart. The two's eyes met.

"I'm no good at this Grace. You should just train someone else. I-I should have watched my step, I should have fought fair, I-"
"You used your environment to your advantage. Fighting fair out here in the Outlands will get your killed." Renata averted her eyes from Grace's fiery gaze. "I saw my student- my friend- try something clever. What's more, you broke your record for this exercise by..." She trailed off, reading her notes, "20 seconds! Look at you!" 

She could see a smile start to form on her face. Grace chuckled, "Now quit all that pouting, and eat! You're too skinny!"

A plastic container with some home recipe landed squarely in Renata's lap. They each popped it open and the smell of grilled ox filled the air.
