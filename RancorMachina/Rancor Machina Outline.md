## Setting:
Over two centuries ago, Earth’s political and economic elite abandoned the planet and relocated to Over Eden, a continent-sized orbital station suspended above a world collapsing under climate catastrophe and pandemic disease. Very little is known about life aboard the station. Communication is scarce, and whatever civilization exists there remains distant and silent while the planet below endures extinction.

Those left behind entered what would later be called the First Extinction Event. Weather systems destabilized beyond recovery. Storms of razor sharp hail tore through cities. Ozone ruptures exposed entire regions to lethal radiation. Electrified superstorms flattened all but the toughest infrastructure and erased populations. Governments fell. Supply chains failed. Billions died. The survivors were forced into a technological and cultural dark age that lasted nearly a century.

As humanity struggled to adapt to a hostile ecosystem, a virus spread across the fractured world, triggering the Second Extinction Event. Entire viral “hot zones” collapsed, their populations dying in the billions. Those who survived did so for because they were naturally immune or they were mutated. Those changed became the first generation of mutants the Sire Mutants, they are larger, mostly animalistic and violently unstable. They were apex predators born from plague and environmental collapse, and for decades they dominated the wastelands between settlements.

From this chaos emerged the Arbiters, elite mech pilots trained to defend what remained of human civilization. Over generations, they became both soldiers and symbols—mobile fortresses that stood between fragile settlements and annihilation. Their greatest victory was the extermination of the Sire Mutants. One by one, the massive, bestial progenitors of the mutant lineage were hunted down and destroyed. Without their Sires, the remaining mutants fragmented into smaller, feral bands of marauders. For the first time in generations, humanity believed extinction had been pushed back.

That hope did not last.

Before reconstruction could truly take hold, two autonomous, AI-controlled war machines emerged. They are known only as The Twins. In their blitzkrieg, they killed every Arbiter but one: Grace Shelby. Unlike the mutants, they do not conquer territory for power, pillage cities for resources, or fight for a god. They eradicate indiscriminately. They appear without warning, level settlements with mechanical precision, and disappear before organized resistance can form.

The Twins have ensured that every attempt at rebuilding ends in devastation. Humanity is now physically diminished and morally exhausted, surviving in scattered enclaves that expect destruction as a matter of course.

Forget the hopes of a better tomorrow, to live in this time is to live under no expectation of happiness or a future, but only extinction. This is a tale of the Third Extinction Event. 

## Theme
_Machines are easier and more efficient, but humanity is resilient and worth the effort; Renata does not succeed despite being human, she succeeds because she's human._
## Renata Honeybone's Positive Change arc
#### The Lie
- Renata believes the lie that she is inadequate and that she could have killed the machines if she was stronger and angrier
- Renata believes that by becoming more intertwined with her machine and removing the human error, she will be stronger
#### The Truth
- Renata learns the truth that what happened was simply out of her control and she's lucky to be alive. Careful preparation and planning is what she needs.
- Throwing away her humanity and removing the human element doesn't make her better than the machines, it simply makes her less human.
#### The Ghost
- Renata is haunted by the fact that Grace Shelby died and she couldn't stop it

## Plot outline
#### Act 1: setup, normal world and call-to-action
- Grace is training Renata to be the next generation of Arbiters, mech pilots who defend humanity from monsters. Their training consists of hunting relatively small but dangerous subterranean monsters.
- Renata is not a good pilot at all and she struggles with this despite Grace's reassuring words.
- We learn Grace is the last Arbiter, a conclave of pilots specializing in mech combat who defended the world from monsters. They were all killed by The Twins.
- The two get a radio alert that The Twins are attacking a nearby city.
- Seeing Renata isn't ready to fight, Grace disables her mech and goes to fight The Twins alone.
- Grace saves several hundred people, but is killed fighting Rancor and Entropy.
- Renata finally catches up, seeing Grace is dead, she tries to fight The Twins too, but she is almost killed, herself.
- Renata crawls inside of Grace's cockpit with her corpse and cries.
- Renata begins to believe the lies: She is innadequate because of her human error. This internalizes as survivors guilt, anger, inadequacy and obsessive training.
- Renata vows revenge against The AI menace.
###### Interlude 1:
- audio logs of how the Arbiters began to form as a group.
#### Act 2: introduction to the truth, all-is-lost breakup arch, and rising action
###### Rising action 1: 
- Renata's mech, is in massive disrepair and is only at the lowest level of functionality, even after using parts from Grace's mech, Kiloton.
- Renata will have to do mech courier work to get enough money to buy better parts. Currently all she has is a machine gun and a solar battery, greatly limiting her speed and combatability.
- Renata's meets Zechariah, a roboticist, who comes with her for company
- Renata goes to a destroyed city (Knot City) to retrieve an abandoned package, she will get paid for the package retrieval but more importantly there is a fusion battery somewhere in the city.
- The Spark is slow and unreliable at this point, so she has to go on foot when she gets to the city.
- The two argue and bicker, but Zech introduces Renata to the truth. But she rejects it, insisting in the lie.
- She and Zech sneak past mutants and load up the recovered package. When retrieving the fusion battery from an old, dead Arbiter mech, she accidentally alerts the mutants.
- The two must escape with the monsters at their back.
- With the added energy load, she can now affix some of the Kiloton's special armor on to her own. she remarks that her mech passingly resembles Graces.
- She trains more when they get back to Mercury's Bluff, her home settlement.
###### Interlude 2:
- audio logs of the Arbiters talking about their exploits in defeating the Sire Mutant Hive Queen
###### Rising action 2:
- second run is guarding a caravan shipment (and carrying a lot of it) from Mercury's Bluff to another settlement, Little Rock.
- She is attacked by a Sire Mutant, a type of mutant thought extinct. She fights and defeats it, drawing on her training.
- Renata affirms her lie after seeing some of the people she was meant to guard die.
- She is able to afford a better weapon and trains harder
- Renata trains obsessively, hunting mutant raider bands and doing jobs.
- She rejects the truth more and trains harder, becoming psychologically intertwined with her mech. She is believing the lie and become less human, becoming mean and cold to her only friend.
###### Breakup:
- Renata goes to a dark place and, after her mech breaks down from too much use, is mean to Zech who is, in turn, tired of her verbal abuse.
- They go their separate ways.
- The all-is-lost! moment.
**Note:** whenever Renata goes further from the truth, she must be punished.
###### Interlude 3:
- audio logs about Grace and how much potential she sees in Renata. She is utterly astonished by her progress in such a short time.
###### Rising action 3:
- She rewatches recordings of the fight and figures out that the The Twins were hacking into her mech and gathering vital information, giving them edges in the fight. This included Ammo Count, audio transcriptions from the cockpit, system inputs, etc. They were even able to stall vital functions for piloting, making her movements more sluggish.
- She goes to Grace's home, lost, convinced that if she fights The Twins again she's dead. But if she doesn't they're all dead anyway. She's faced with her own mortality.
- This is the point where the lie and the truth can no longer coincide and she must choose who she will be.
- She goes to Grace's home, looking for guidance. she finds hours of audio logs of the Arbiters. These are the logs that have been interluded through the book. It reminds her of how human Grace and the others were. That there is still beauty in the world worth fighting for. 
- She guts her mech's computer systems, making it fully manual. This is like driving stick shift with no power steering. possible but extremely challenging.
###### Reunion:
- Renata apologizes. She cannot defeat the Twins without his help physically and emotionally.
- Zech eventually helps her see that it was not her fault. She finally accepts the truth.
- She accepts that it wasn't her fault and she's lucky to be alive. Additionally, becoming colder is not the same as becoming stronger.
#### Act 3: climax, affirmation of the truth, and resolution
- Renata faces off against The Twins when they emerge again.
- She is a different person, but they are exactly the same. This is where she affirms her truth. against them.
- The Twins are a dark mirror to renata. Internally renata risked becoming cold and inhuman through her trauma and obsession. Externally, she began to integrate too closely to her machine. Inversely, the Twins represent a visage of what she could have become.
- She battles both of them, killing Entropy first.
- Almost dies to Rancor, but as her mech is destroyed, she is able to jump into his cockpit.
- She sees that he isn't an AI, he is a man with no arms or legs, directly integrated into the mech. The symbol on his gear indicates that he was the leader of the Arbiters.
- She doesn't win despite being human, she wins because she is human.
